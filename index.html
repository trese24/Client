VisionABLE Manila

is an advocacy platform that promotes inclusive and accessible signage in Metro Manila for people with visual impairments especially those with blurred vision, color blindness, and other low vision conditions.

Through documentary storytelling, user-submitted experiences, and downloadable design guides, we aim to raise awareness, influence better urban design, and empower both designers and government agencies to take action
We believe that accessibility starts with visibility and every sign should be seen, read, and understood by all.

Why Accessible Signage Matters: Seeing the City Through Inclusive Design

Add this ( Giving attention to public signage is important because signs guide people through everyday life especially
in complex environments like roads, transport terminals, hospitals, and government buildings.
Here’s why it matters) under “why accessible signage matters” Seeing the City through Inclusive Design

Safety Clear and accessible signage prevents accidents. For people with visual impairments, good signage can mean the difference between a safe commute and a dangerous situation like missing exits, crossing roads unsafely, or getting lost in unfamiliar spaces.

Navigation and Confidence Public signs help everyone find their way. But for persons with low vision or other disabilities, poor signage becomes a barrier. Accessible signs restore a person’s freedom to move independently, without needing constant assistance.

Inclusion and Equal Access When signs are designed only for people with perfect vision, it excludes a large part of the population including the elderly, people with disabilities, and even those in low-light or high-glare conditions. Inclusive signage ensures everyone is considered.

It’s a Right Not a Privilege Under international standards like the UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (CRPD), accessible information and signage are part of every citizen’s right to access public services.

Better Urban Design Cities that invest in inclusive signage are more organized, user-friendly, and future ready. It reflects a commitment to universal design, where the environment works for all people, regardless of ability.
